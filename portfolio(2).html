The Caravan
Magazine

DisciplinePrint · Publication · Typography
Year2025
TypePersonal Project
Cover & Master Spread Visual 01 — 02
Overview

The Caravan is India's foremost magazine of long-form journalism and narrative non-fiction. This project reimagined its visual identity — rebuilding the layout system from the ground up to honour the density and rigour of its writing.

The brief was self-initiated: to find a typographic and spatial language that felt contemporary without losing the tactile gravitas that print demands. Every decision, from column widths to leading ratios, was made in service of extended reading.

"Typography at the scale of long-form journalism must breathe — but it must also hold."
Feature Opener — Issue LayoutVisual 03
Body Copy & Grid SystemVisual 04
Approach

Typographic System

Two typefaces anchor the system: a contemporary grotesque for headlines and navigation, and a high-contrast serif for body text — chosen for its performance at small sizes across long passages.

A strict 6-column grid governs every spread. Section openers break it deliberately, using full-bleed image placements and oversized numeral folios to signal hierarchy without relying on colour.

Grid & Rhythm

Baseline grids were locked to 14pt — tight enough to maintain density, loose enough for images to breathe. Margins were widened beyond conventional magazine standards, giving the long-form text the white space it deserves.

Pull-quote TreatmentVisual 05
Caption SystemVisual 06
Section DividerVisual 07
Outcome

The redesign produced a coherent, scalable system — one that can accommodate the full range of The Caravan's editorial formats: the 10,000-word investigation, the photo essay, the short dispatch, and the back-of-book reviews.

The exercise reinforced a core belief: great publication design is invisible. It creates the conditions for reading without announcing itself.

Unbound &
Untethered

DisciplineResearch · Publication · Visual Design
Year2025
Pages80 pp.
Publication Cover & Opening SpreadVisual 01 — 02
Overview

Unbound & Untethered is an 80-page research publication produced for the India Foundation for the Arts (IFA). It explores how artists, thinkers, and practitioners have navigated freedom and belonging — across disciplines, geographies, and time.

The visual challenge: how to hold archival material alongside contemporary essay writing, without the design flattening either into mere illustration.

"The archive should feel unearthed, not curated. The design must carry that tension."
Archival Image SpreadVisual 03
Essay OpenerVisual 04
Process

Research & Visual Language

The process began with an extensive audit of the archival material — photographs, handwritten letters, printed ephemera — to understand the tonal range of the content before a single layout was conceived.

A visual language emerged: raw-edged image placements, deliberate whitespace as expressive tool, and a type system that distinguished between voices — the scholar, the artist, the archivist — through weight and size alone.

Expressive Layout

Rather than imposing a rigid grid across all 80 pages, the layout system was designed to flex. Essay sections follow a strict two-column structure. Archival sections break free — images overlapping text blocks, captions running in the margins, pages given over entirely to a single document.

Document SpreadVisual 05
Annotation DetailVisual 06
Back MatterVisual 07
Outcome

The publication was received as a serious piece of design scholarship — one that used the visual form of the book not as a container but as an argument. The tension between rigour and expressiveness became the work's defining quality.

Delivered as both a print edition and a digital PDF, the design was built to survive both formats without compromise.

Google
Pay

DisciplineInteraction Design · Product · Research
Year2025
PlatformMobile — Android / iOS
Onboarding Flow — Final ScreensVisual 01 — 03
Overview

Google Pay in India has one of the highest first-time user drop-off rates in the payments category. This project targeted the onboarding and first-transaction flows — the critical 10 minutes in which a new user either commits to the app or abandons it.

The work was grounded in primary field research across three cities: Pune, Nagpur, and Aurangabad — spanning users from college students to small-business owners, many encountering UPI for the first time.

"First-time users don't fear technology — they fear making a mistake they can't undo."
Field Research — User InterviewsVisual 04
Affinity Mapping & InsightsVisual 05
Process

Research Findings

Field research surfaced three dominant pain points: confusing UPI ID entry, anxiety around sending to wrong recipients, and no legible confirmation state before a transaction finalised. These weren't usability issues — they were trust issues.

Design Decisions

Key interventions included: a persistent "undo window" displayed immediately after a transaction is sent; real-name verification shown before the send confirmation; and a simplified first-run experience that walks users through a ₹1 test transaction before they send money to contacts.

Wireframe — Send FlowVisual 06
High-Fidelity — ConfirmationVisual 07
Prototype — Test TransactionVisual 08
Outcome

Prototype testing with 12 participants showed a significant reduction in hesitation time at the confirmation step, and zero instances of the "wrong recipient" anxiety that appeared consistently in baseline testing.

The project demonstrated that the most meaningful UX improvements in fintech aren't visual — they're about sequencing, feedback, and the language of trust.

Final UI — Key ScreensVisual 01 — 04
Overview

A complete, end-to-end product design process — from initial discovery research through to a polished, handoff-ready high-fidelity prototype. The focus was on designing for accessibility and intuitive interaction patterns across a complex multi-step workflow.

The work demonstrates the full arc of product thinking: not just interface design, but the decisions that precede it — what to build, for whom, and why it will be used.

"Good product design makes complex things feel simple — not by hiding complexity, but by sequencing it."
User Research & Journey MappingVisual 05
Information ArchitectureVisual 06
Process

Discovery & Research

Structured user interviews and competitive analysis identified gaps in the existing landscape. Journey mapping sessions surfaced the emotional arc of users — moments of delight, confusion, and abandonment.

From Wireframes to High-Fidelity

Low-fidelity wireframes were tested early with representative users, allowing structural problems to be solved before visual design began. The high-fidelity UI was built with a component library that prioritised accessibility: minimum contrast ratios of 4.5:1, focus states for keyboard navigation, and touch targets sized to WCAG 2.1 AA standards.

Component LibraryVisual 07
Prototype — Core FlowVisual 08
Accessibility AnnotationsVisual 09
Outcome

The final deliverable included a fully annotated Figma prototype, a component library with 60+ components, and a handoff document covering spacing, motion, and interaction specifications.

The project reinforced the principle that accessibility is not a constraint on good design — it is the measure of it.
